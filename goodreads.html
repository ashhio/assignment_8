Goodreads

Tweaking a site to improve community

Overview

Goodreads is a site that helps people find and share books they love, but users are dissatisfied with Goodreads due to functional limitations and outdated design. In this project, I redesigned portions of the site, hoping to touch on some simple changes that could improve Goodreads’ overall experience.

The Problem

Goodreads has a monopoly over all other book-discussion sites. It has great potential and a wide user base, but according to this article, Goodreads has not delivered on its promises. Functionality issues and limitations leave users unsatisfied and wanting more. The founders of Goodreads intended the site to be a place to discover books based on what your friends are reading, but book recommendations are largely based on what is popular. Also, there is no option to make private shelves, annotations, or ratings. Public reviews are dubious and filled with spam. The lack of website support for organizations, clubs, and public figures prevents Goodreads users from creating a strong community. Finally, the website design is outdated and neglected.

Many of the issues listed above cannot be solved with UI improvements. For instance, the recommendation system needs a technical overhaul to satisfy users. However, I believe that with some tweaks, it is possible to transform Goodreads from a book list-making site to a place that fosters a community of readers and offers good recommendations. These changes include fostering connections between users that share a similar taste in books. Hopefully, by promoting groups and followers, making books the focal point of a user’s profile, and updating the UI, Goodreads will become a more enriching experience.

Conclusion

I created a mockup of some Goodreads pages to illustrate ways to improve the website while considering user complaints. Although many issues users have with Goodreads must be addressed via functionality improvements, there are some UI tweaks shown in the mockup that puts forth books, the community, and connecting with other users as the focal point of the site. However, Goodreads is a complex product that has a diverse user base. More pages must be created and tested to determine the effectiveness of these ideas.
